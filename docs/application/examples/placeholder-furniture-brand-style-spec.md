# Placeholder Furniture Brand Style Spec

This is a fictive SKU-driven furniture-brand style spec meant to test normative information structure for future AI-native asset workflows.

## Brand Premise

Northline House is a placeholder contemporary furniture brand focused on calm, architectural product presentation for ecommerce and catalog use. The visual direction is quiet, material-led, and bright without becoming lifestyle-editorial or decorative for its own sake.

## Input Categories

- product / SKU data: product class, dimensions, finish, upholstery, colorway, and merchandising identifiers
- PD references: CAD views, line-sheet references, and product-dev imagery that clarify silhouette, proportions, and construction details
- style references: approved examples that show the intended tonal range, lighting character, scene restraint, and merchandising posture

## Bounded Creative Discretion

Creative choice is allowed in how scenes are simplified, how supporting props are minimized, and how lighting is balanced, but only inside defined brand boundaries. The goal is controlled variation, not open-ended reinterpretation of the product or the brand.

## Shared Constraints

- composition / camera / crop: products should read clearly, stay proportionally credible, and avoid expressive lens distortion or aggressive crops
- environment / scene rules: environments should feel quiet, usable, and secondary to the product rather than styled as narrative sets
- styling rules: props and textiles may support scale and context, but must not become the visual subject
- fidelity rules: finish, silhouette, seams, legs, joinery, and material behavior must remain faithful to product references
- technical output requirements: outputs should remain consistent enough for governed ecommerce and catalog use across SKUs

## Product-Class Shot Guidance

### Sofa

- one clean front or front-three-quarter hero
- one side or angled view that preserves seat depth and arm profile
- one detail crop where material or construction needs legibility

### Dining Chair

- one front or front-three-quarter hero
- one profile view that preserves back shape and leg stance
- one close view where upholstery, wood finish, or joinery needs legibility

## Output Expectations

Governed outputs should be legible, brand-consistent, and SKU-specific enough to support controlled downstream use. The output set should make product class, finish, and proportions clear without relying on narrative styling.

## What This Example Is Not

- real Netsuite or Airtable ingestion
- prompt libraries
- schema formalization
- runtime orchestration
- UI design
- broad furniture taxonomy build-out

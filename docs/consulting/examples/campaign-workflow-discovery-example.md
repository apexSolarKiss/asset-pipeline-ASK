# Campaign Workflow Discovery Example

This example applies the workflow discovery worksheet to an abstract campaign-style visual asset workflow.

## Intent

Briefly describe what this workflow is trying to achieve and how that purpose is meant to be expressed.

### Business Intent

- Build a coordinated campaign asset set that can support awareness, engagement, and release-period conversion goals across more than one distribution surface
- Establish a campaign frame that can stay recognizable even when individual assets vary in composition or emphasis
- Deliver governed assets within a limited campaign window without collapsing creative work into a purely fixed production pattern

### Creative Intent

- Express the campaign through a distinct seasonal mood rather than a single literal motif
- Allow multiple visual interpretations so long as they remain coherent as one campaign family
- Balance atmosphere and expressiveness with enough clarity to support communication goals

## Inputs

Briefly identify what enters the workflow as material, parameters, and context.

### Source Materials

- Existing identity assets and previously approved visual elements
- Prior campaign references used as continuity cues rather than direct templates
- A pool of approved still-image source materials available for recomposition and adaptation

### Supplied Parameters

- Campaign window, required delivery timing, and expected output count
- Target format classes and priority distribution surfaces
- Required identifiers and essential informational elements that must appear where relevant

### Contextual Instructions

- Emphasize renewal, immediacy, and seasonal transition without overcommitting to one visual device
- Favor emotionally resonant compositions over rigid uniformity
- Preserve continuity across assets while allowing asset-level interpretation

## Constraints

Briefly identify what the workflow must remain within.

### Hard Requirements

- Outputs must remain usable across the designated formats
- Required campaign identifiers and essential information must remain present and legible
- Final assets must stay within the approved review and delivery window

### Bounded Discretion

- Composition, tonal emphasis, and pacing of visual focus may vary when doing so strengthens campaign expression
- Thematic interpretation may differ between assets if the set still reads as one campaign family
- Expressive treatment is allowed, but not at the expense of clarity or required informational presence

### Exclusion Rules

- Visual directions that break continuity with the broader campaign frame are not allowed
- Unreviewed source material and unsupported claims are excluded
- Creative freedom does not extend to omitting required identifiers or distorting essential information

## Orchestration

Briefly describe how work moves through the system.

- The workflow begins with campaign framing, input assembly, and selection of an initial direction range
- A small set of candidate directions is developed and narrowed through review rather than fixed upfront
- Approved directions are extended into multiple outputs, then routed through revision and final approval

## Outputs

Briefly describe what the workflow is expected to produce.

- A coordinated set of campaign assets prepared for more than one distribution surface
- Review-ready variants where comparison is needed before a final direction is selected
- Final approved outputs that remain connected as a campaign family without requiring identical treatment

## Governance

Briefly describe how the workflow is checked, approved, and exception-handled.

### Validation

- Outputs are checked for legibility, required informational presence, and campaign-level coherence
- Variation is evaluated to ensure it stays within the intended interpretive range rather than becoming unrelated creative drift
- Source use and claims are checked against approved bounds

### Approval

- Human review determines whether a direction is both campaign-coherent and fit for release
- Approval matters first at direction selection and again at final asset release
- Approval reflects creative judgment, communication risk, and release readiness at the same time

### Override

- Exception review is available when a promising output exceeds normal interpretive bounds
- Scope may be narrowed under schedule pressure, but only through explicit approval rather than informal drift
- Recurring exceptions tend to appear where creative discretion and governance thresholds are not yet fully aligned

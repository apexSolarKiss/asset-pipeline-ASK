# Campaign Workflow Redesign Example

This example applies the workflow redesign brief to the abstract campaign workflow diagnosis case.

## Redesign Aim

- Make campaign-direction freedom more legible without reducing the workflow to a rigid production system
- Preserve expressive latitude while tightening the points where review, validation, and exception handling become explicit

## Proposed Target-State Shape

### Intent

- Keep business intent and creative intent distinct, but make their practical tradeoff boundary explicit at key review points

### Inputs

- Preserve the distinction between source materials, supplied parameters, and contextual instructions so interpretive guidance does not silently become an unbounded input bucket

### Constraints

- Keep hard requirements stable while defining bounded discretion more explicitly around permissible variation and campaign coherence

### Orchestration

- Preserve exploratory early phases, but make the transition from direction exploration to governed output production more explicit

### Outputs

- Treat outputs as a coherent campaign family with a clearer distinction between review-ready variants and final governed deliverables

### Governance

- Separate validation, approval, and override more clearly so judgment remains central without leaving exception logic informal

## Key Shifts From Current State

- Campaign-direction exploration should remain open, but the criteria for convergence should become clearer
- Governance should become more explicit at the points where interpretation turns into approved direction and where approved direction turns into final output
- Recurrent ambiguity between contextual guidance, creative discretion, and exception handling should move into named structural boundaries

## Expected Benefits

- Better clarity about where expressive freedom is encouraged and where it is bounded
- More consistent review behavior across related campaign outputs
- Stronger continuity between creative variation and governed release quality

## Design Tradeoffs

- Greater structural clarity may reduce some informal flexibility during direction development
- More explicit governance boundaries may make some creative escalations slower
- Tighter distinctions between review variants and final outputs may add modest process overhead

## Preconditions / Dependencies

- Agreement is needed on what campaign coherence means at a practical review level
- The workflow needs a clearer shared understanding of when a direction is exploratory versus approval-ready
- Override conditions need enough definition to distinguish true exceptions from normal creative variation

## Open Design Questions

- How explicit should intent tradeoff logic become without over-formalizing creative work?
- What degree of output variation is desirable before the workflow starts to behave as multiple workflows rather than one campaign family?
- Which governance judgments should remain intentionally interpretive even in a more explicit target-state model?

# Workflow Discovery Worksheet

This worksheet is a provisional discovery aid for mapping a visual asset workflow using the repository's current working ontology.

## Intent

Briefly describe what this workflow is trying to achieve and how that purpose is meant to be expressed.

### Business Intent

- What is this workflow meant to achieve?
- What business outcome does it support?
- Where do business priorities become explicit?

### Creative Intent

- How is that purpose meant to be expressed?
- What kind of variation or interpretation is desirable?
- Where do creative priorities align or conflict with business priorities?

## Inputs

Briefly identify what enters the workflow as material, parameters, and context.

### Source Materials

- What materials or assets enter the workflow?
- Which inputs are reused versus created for this workflow?
- Which materials are treated as authoritative?

### Supplied Parameters

- What structured parameters or requested values enter the workflow?
- Which parameters vary by asset, batch, or channel?
- Which inputs are highly repeatable versus case-specific?

### Contextual Instructions

- What contextual instructions or framing enter the workflow?
- Which inputs are interpretive rather than strictly structured?
- Where does workflow mode seem to change the weight of contextual guidance?

## Constraints

Briefly identify what the workflow must remain within.

### Hard Requirements

- What is non-negotiable?
- Which requirements must always be satisfied?
- Which requirements shape the workflow regardless of mode?

### Bounded Discretion

- Where is variation allowed?
- What kinds of judgment are expected inside defined bounds?
- How wide or narrow is the permitted range of interpretation?

### Exclusion Rules

- What is explicitly disallowed?
- What kinds of outputs, methods, or inputs are ruled out?
- Which exclusions are stable versus situational?

## Orchestration

Briefly describe how work moves through the system.

- How does work move from intake to output?
- Where do major handoffs, loops, or review cycles occur?
- Which parts of the flow feel exploratory versus repeatable?

## Outputs

Briefly describe what the workflow is expected to produce.

- What outputs are required?
- How much variation between outputs is acceptable?
- Which outputs are final versus primarily for review or comparison?

## Governance

Briefly describe how the workflow is checked, approved, and exception-handled.

### Validation

- How is sufficiency checked?
- What makes an output valid enough to proceed?
- Which checks are judgment-heavy versus more rule-based?

### Approval

- Who approves the work?
- At what points does approval matter?
- Where does approval reflect taste, risk, or release readiness?

### Override

- How are exceptions handled?
- Who can authorize deviation from the normal pattern?
- Which exceptions recur often enough to reveal workflow-stream variation?

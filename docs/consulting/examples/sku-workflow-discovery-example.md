# SKU Workflow Discovery Example

This example applies the workflow discovery worksheet to an abstract modular, repeatable SKU-style visual asset workflow.

## Intent

Briefly describe what this workflow is trying to achieve and how that purpose is meant to be expressed.

### Business Intent

- Produce a repeatable asset set across a large item range with predictable quality, coverage, and turnaround
- Ensure that each item and required variant is represented consistently enough for comparison, selection, and downstream reuse
- Minimize unnecessary variation so the workflow remains scalable, auditable, and easy to validate in batch form

### Creative Intent

- Maintain a clean, credible presentation standard that feels intentional rather than mechanically uneven
- Allow only limited visual interpretation where it improves legibility, fit, or clarity across item differences
- Preserve visual coherence across the full set without introducing unnecessary stylistic drift

## Inputs

Briefly identify what enters the workflow as material, parameters, and context.

### Source Materials

- Approved source images or renderable item views associated with each item record
- Reusable background, framing, and layout components intended for repeatable application
- Supporting descriptive records that clarify item identity and required coverage

### Supplied Parameters

- Item-level identifiers, variant attributes, and required asset counts
- Approved dimensions, aspect classes, and required delivery surfaces
- Coverage rules indicating which item and variant combinations must be represented

### Contextual Instructions

- Favor consistency, comparability, and legibility over expressive differentiation
- Treat variation as a response to item-specific needs rather than as an independent creative opportunity
- Preserve a neutral presentation standard that can scale across a large set

## Constraints

Briefly identify what the workflow must remain within.

### Hard Requirements

- Every required item and variant must receive the required asset coverage
- Outputs must preserve accurate representation and required identifying information
- Asset dimensions, framing rules, and delivery formats must remain within the approved target set

### Bounded Discretion

- Minor adjustments to crop, scale, spacing, or emphasis are allowed when needed to preserve clarity across differing item shapes
- Presentation may adapt slightly when item-specific needs require it, so long as the overall system remains coherent
- Small interpretive choices are acceptable only when they improve consistency of outcome rather than stylistic variance

### Exclusion Rules

- Item-to-item stylistic drift that weakens comparability is not allowed
- Required variants, identifiers, or mandatory views may not be omitted
- Improvisational treatments that make outputs feel like separate presentation systems are excluded

## Orchestration

Briefly describe how work moves through the system.

- The workflow begins with structured item intake, parameter assembly, and confirmation of required coverage
- Approved presentation rules are applied across the item set, with limited adjustment where needed for clarity
- Outputs are generated in batch form, validated for coverage and conformity, then routed for exception review only where necessary

## Outputs

Briefly describe what the workflow is expected to produce.

- A repeatable set of still-image outputs covering the required items and variants across more than one delivery surface
- A complete output set with predictable formatting, consistent representation, and comparable presentation
- Explicitly separated exception cases where normal presentation rules cannot be applied cleanly

## Governance

Briefly describe how the workflow is checked, approved, and exception-handled.

### Validation

- Coverage is checked across all required items and variants before release
- Outputs are checked for dimensional conformity, framing compliance, and representation accuracy
- Consistency is checked across the batch so exceptions are explicit rather than accidental

### Approval

- Human review confirms that the presentation standard is being applied consistently and that flagged exceptions are legitimate
- Approval matters at batch-level sufficiency and at exception resolution points rather than primarily at stylistic direction stages
- Approval reflects release readiness, consistency, and representation accuracy more than expressive judgment

### Override

- Explicit exception approval is used when a specific item cannot meet the normal presentation pattern without loss of clarity
- Missing scope may be deferred only through explicit decision rather than silent omission
- Recurring exceptions tend to surface where the standard system does not yet account for real item variation

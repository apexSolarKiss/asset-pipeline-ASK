# Milestone 2 Four-Mode Cross-Mode Pressure Plateau

This note records the plateau where the repo had pressure-tested the shared model across four worked workflow modes.

## What This Milestone Established

- four worked-example modes were available for comparison
- the shared model could be compared across SKU-driven product imagery, collection / merchandising, marketing / message-driven, and brand campaign / editorial workflows
- single-item truth, relational / collection truth, message-bearing discipline, and campaign / editorial coherence were distinguishable pressures
- review-artifact burden appeared different across modes
- shared constraints and mode-weighted constraints became more legible
- the comparison layer became more durable without hardening the modes into final taxonomy
- this plateau predates later Airtable prototype work and the output-slot architecture chain

## What It Did Not Establish

- Airtable prototype behavior
- output-slot structure
- final schema doctrine
- final workflow-mode taxonomy
- production readiness

## What Remains Out Of Scope

- schema design from the four-mode comparison alone
- workflow-mode hardening
- validator or runtime expansion
- treating any one worked mode as the model for all others
- production-readiness claims

## Next Pressure Point

The next pressure after this plateau is translating cross-mode conceptual pressure into bounded prototype surfaces without hardening the modes into final schema.

# Placeholder Campaign Workflow Packet Example

This is a single provisional workflow packet example for one seasonal still-image campaign inside the `brand campaign / editorial` creative stream.

## Campaign Context

- campaign label: `Example Lifestyle Brand / Early Autumn Reset`
- campaign window: late-summer to early-autumn still-image launch period
- campaign purpose: produce a small coordinated asset set that can support awareness, seasonal relevance, and light conversion intent across a small set of controlled surfaces
- governing campaign frame: calm seasonal transition, renewed routine, and everyday uplift without collapsing into one literal motif

## Brand And Directional Context

This packet assumes `Example Lifestyle Brand` as an obviously placeholder brand label rather than a real brand system. The directional context is warm, pared back, and contemporary, with enough seasonal character to feel campaign-specific but not so much aesthetic specificity that the packet becomes a hidden style guide.

The campaign should read as recognizably part of one brief-shaped family. Directional consistency matters, but strict visual sameness is not the goal. The governing pressure is campaign coherence across a small output set rather than SKU-level comparability across many assets.

## Bounded Creative Discretion

Creative choice is allowed in composition, crop, tonal balance, pacing of visual emphasis, and how strongly seasonal cues are carried in a given asset. Those choices must remain inside the campaign frame and must not drift into unrelated visual directions or obscure required message-bearing elements.

Variation across assets is acceptable where it strengthens the campaign family rather than weakening it. The packet assumes a wider interpretive range than the existing SKU-driven furniture example, but that range remains bounded by continuity, clarity, and reviewability.

## Constraints

- campaign-frame continuity constraints: outputs should remain recognizably part of the same seasonal campaign family even when composition or emphasis differs from asset to asset
- message-bearing signal constraints: required campaign identifiers, tagline presence where applicable, and any review-relevant directional signals must remain legible enough to support governed use
- expressive-range constraints: variation in mood, crop, or emphasis is allowed only where it stays inside the approved seasonal frame and does not become an unrelated creative direction
- surface-usability constraints: outputs should remain usable across the intended small set of surfaces without losing clarity, continuity, or required campaign identifiers, message-bearing elements, or review-relevant signals
- source-bound constraints: the packet should rely only on approved source materials and approved campaign-direction inputs rather than unsupported additions or unreviewed claims

## Required Output Set

- governed outputs: one primary hero image for the campaign landing surface, one secondary crop or variant for a tighter promotional surface, and one supporting asset that extends the same campaign family without repeating the hero exactly
- review artifacts: one side-by-side review board showing the candidate asset family together, and one light annotation layer or review note artifact where directional concerns need to be called out before approval

## Requested Production Objective

Produce a small governed still-image campaign set for `Example Lifestyle Brand / Early Autumn Reset` that reads as campaign-coherent, directionally bounded, and ready to move through the current verification chain toward release-readiness review.

The packet should make it possible to test whether continuity and coherence pressures can hold the same structural place that product truth and comparability held in the SKU-driven furniture example, while keeping review artifacts explicit enough to support judgment-bearing approval.

## What This Packet Is Not

- a campaign style-spec build-out
- a campaign-brief-to-seam bridge design
- prompt libraries or prompt templates
- schema formalization
- runtime orchestration
- new seam design
- broader taxonomy settlement
- reusable packet standards

# Campaign Workflow Diagnosis Example

This example applies the workflow diagnosis memo to the abstract campaign workflow discovery case.

## Workflow Snapshot

- A high-variance campaign workflow oriented around expressive coherence rather than strict repeatability
- Observed mode is more high-variance than repeatable, though still bounded by release and information requirements
- The current operational frame combines creative exploration, review-driven narrowing, and governed multi-output delivery

## Core Findings

### Intent

- Business and creative intent are both legible, but their tradeoff logic remains implicit rather than formally defined
- The workflow depends on expressive latitude, yet still carries delivery and communication obligations that constrain it

### Inputs

- Contextual instructions carry unusually high weight relative to supplied parameters
- Source materials are reused as continuity anchors, but not as rigid templates

### Constraints

- Hard requirements are clear around timing, format usability, and informational presence
- Bounded discretion is active and important, but its practical edges are enforced more through review than through explicit modeling

### Orchestration

- The flow is exploratory early and convergent later, with direction selection acting as the main narrowing point
- Review loops are a core structural feature rather than an exception path

### Outputs

- The workflow expects a family of related outputs rather than a single standardized result
- Review-ready variants play a meaningful role even though outputs are not yet internally modeled in more detail

### Governance

- Governance is judgment-heavy and tied closely to campaign coherence, not only rule checking
- Override exists, but it currently behaves more like escalated interpretation than a clearly formalized exception path

## Key Tensions

- Creative latitude is necessary to the workflow, but its governing limits are not fully explicit until review
- Contextual instructions and bounded discretion are distinct in theory, yet can blur in practice during direction development
- Governance and orchestration remain partially entangled because review is both a flow stage and a judgment boundary

## Working Diagnosis

- The workflow is structurally strong where it separates expressive campaign intent from non-negotiable release conditions
- It is less stable where interpretive freedom, review authority, and exception handling rely on practice more than explicit structure

## Advisory Priorities

- Make the boundary between contextual guidance and bounded discretion more explicit
- Clarify where campaign-direction approval ends and final-output approval begins
- Define override conditions more clearly so exceptional creative decisions do not depend on informal escalation

## Open Questions

- Should business and creative intent remain co-equal in this workflow, or should precedence become more explicit under time pressure?
- How much output variation is genuinely beneficial before campaign coherence starts to weaken?
- Which governance thresholds should remain judgment-based, and which should become more explicit?

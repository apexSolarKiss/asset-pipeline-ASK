# Placeholder Marketing Message-Driven Packet To Seam Chain Map

## Purpose

This note is a conceptual bridge between the placeholder marketing / message-driven workflow packet artifact and the current deterministic seam chain.

Its purpose is to pressure-test whether a targeted promotional communication set can remain structurally legible across the current seams without collapsing into campaign looseness, single-SKU documentation, schema formalization, or runtime language.

## Ingest

The marketing / message-driven packet enters the seam chain first as ingest-facing information. Message context, approved product inputs, approved brand inputs, approved promotional inputs, declared communication boundaries, bounded creative discretion, and review-relevant inputs belong here because they define what the controlled promotional set is trying to communicate and what later work must remain within.

In this message-driven case, ingest carries forward not just product presence or broad brand direction, but the targeted communication purpose itself. The important pressure is that the offer, message-bearing elements, and communication boundaries are declared early enough that later seams do not have to infer the promotional frame from asset styling alone.

## Constraints Carried Across The Chain

The marketing / message-driven packet acts as a normative constraint layer across transformation and output behavior. Message-bearing signal persistence, targeted-purpose discipline, bounded promotional variation, surface-fit usability, and enough product truth to avoid obvious distortion should shape what later stages are allowed to produce, but they do not become runtime orchestration, channel taxonomy, or generalized marketing schema by themselves.

This bridge should make clear that message variation is allowed only inside one controlled communication frame. The governing question is not whether the set becomes louder or broader, but whether the promotional message remains legible, bounded, and governable across the asset family.

## Output Expectations

At the output stage, the marketing / message-driven packet informs whether governed outputs read as a valid, usable promotional communication set. The relevant question is whether the set preserves its message-bearing signals, remains inside its targeted purpose, and stays usable across the intended controlled surfaces without losing promotional discipline or drifting into a different workflow shape.

Review artifacts may support message-legibility review and comparison here, but they should not collapse the seam asymmetry between governed output sufficiency and later readiness-to-present.

## Release-Readiness Expectations

At release-readiness, the marketing / message-driven packet matters insofar as it helps determine whether the controlled promotional set is ready to be presented for approval. The relevant question is whether the candidate set remains legible, bounded, and reviewable enough to cross the approval-facing boundary as one governed communication set.

That is still narrower than automating approval or marketing judgment. Release-readiness tests readiness to present the set for approval, not whether the repo has encoded final decision logic for what counts as good promotion.

## What This Does Not Do

- define a channel taxonomy
- formalize schema or field catalogs
- introduce runtime or orchestration language
- define new seam logic or a new seam
- automate approval or marketing judgment
- define a generalized marketing framework

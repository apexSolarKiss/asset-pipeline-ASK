# Example Campaign Workflow Instance

This example illustrates one abstract workflow instance using the repository's current working ontology. It is intended to pressure-test the model against a high-variance, high-discretion campaign workflow without introducing brand-specific assumptions.

This abstract example is based on [`docs/workflow-instance-template.md`](workflow-instance-template.md).

## Intent

Describe what the workflow is meant to achieve and how that purpose is meant to be expressed.

### Business Intent

- Produce a coordinated seasonal campaign asset set that can support awareness, engagement, and conversion-oriented messaging across more than one generic distribution surface.
- Establish a recognizable thematic frame that can be repeated across multiple deliverables without requiring identical visual treatment in every output.
- Deliver assets that can be reviewed, approved, and adapted within a bounded campaign window.

### Creative Intent

- Express the campaign through a distinct seasonal mood that feels current, evocative, and visually cohesive without collapsing into a single literal interpretation.
- Allow multiple compositional and tonal interpretations so long as the outputs remain recognizably part of the same campaign family.
- Balance atmosphere, clarity, and legibility rather than optimizing exclusively for one of those qualities.

## Inputs

Describe what is supplied to the workflow as material, parameters, and context.

### Source Materials

- Existing visual identity assets suitable for reuse at a general level.
- Prior campaign references used only as inspiration and continuity cues.
- A reusable pool of approved still-image source materials and design elements.

### Supplied Parameters

- Campaign window and target delivery timing.
- Required output count and a small set of target format classes.
- Priority surfaces for initial distribution.

### Contextual Instructions

- Emphasize seasonal transition, renewal, and immediacy without relying on a single literal motif.
- Favor emotionally resonant compositions over rigidly uniform layouts.
- Preserve a sense of campaign continuity while allowing asset-level interpretation.

## Constraints

Describe what the workflow must remain within, including non-negotiables, bounded freedom, and explicit exclusions.

### Hard Requirements

- Outputs must remain usable across the designated format classes.
- Required campaign identifiers and essential informational elements must remain present and legible where applicable.
- Final assets must stay within the approved timing and review window.

### Bounded Discretion

- Composition, cropping, pacing of visual emphasis, and tonal balance may vary across assets when doing so strengthens the campaign expression.
- Thematic interpretation may differ between assets so long as the overall campaign remains coherent.
- Visual treatment may be expressive, but not at the expense of clarity or required informational presence.

### Exclusion Rules

- Do not introduce visual directions that sever continuity with the broader campaign frame.
- Do not rely on unreviewed source material or unsupported claims.
- Do not treat interpretive freedom as permission to omit required identifiers or distort essential information.

## Orchestration

Describe the major flow of work, transformations, or handoffs at a high level.

- Establish campaign intent and assemble the working input set.
- Generate and refine a small set of candidate directions within the allowed creative range.
- Select a direction family for extension into multiple governed outputs.
- Adapt and refine outputs for the intended distribution surfaces.
- Route outputs through review, revision, and final approval.

## Outputs

Describe the intended governed outputs of the workflow.

- A coordinated set of still-image campaign assets prepared for more than one generic distribution surface.
- Supporting review-ready variants where comparison is needed before final approval.
- Final approved outputs that remain recognizably connected while not necessarily identical in composition or emphasis.

## Governance

Describe how the workflow is validated, approved, and exceptionally overridden when needed.

### Validation

- Check that each output satisfies required informational presence, format usability, and campaign-level coherence.
- Check that variation between assets remains inside the allowed interpretive range rather than becoming unrelated creative directions.
- Check that source use and claims remain within approved bounds.

### Approval

- Human review confirms that the selected direction is both campaign-coherent and fit for release.
- Human review confirms that expressive variation has strengthened the campaign rather than weakened clarity or continuity.
- Final approval is granted only after required revisions are resolved.

### Override

- If a promising output exceeds normal interpretive bounds but remains strategically valuable, it may be escalated for explicit exception review.
- If schedule pressure requires narrowing scope, output count or variation range may be reduced through explicit approval rather than informal drift.
- Any override must be treated as a conscious exception to normal governance, not as an unrecorded bypass of review.

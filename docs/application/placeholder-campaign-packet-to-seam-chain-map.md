# Placeholder Campaign Packet To Seam Chain Map

## Purpose

This note is a conceptual bridge between the placeholder campaign workflow packet artifact and the current deterministic seam chain.

Its purpose is to test whether a looser `brand campaign / editorial` creative stream can still be made structurally legible across the current seams without collapsing into vague prose, premature schema, or runtime language.

## Ingest

The campaign packet enters the seam chain first as ingest-facing information. Campaign context, the governing campaign frame, approved directional context, bounded creative discretion, required campaign identifiers, message-bearing elements, and review-relevant inputs belong here because they define what the asset family is trying to achieve and what later work must remain within.

In this campaign-side case, ingest does not establish one product-truth anchor in the way the furniture example did. Instead, it carries forward the brief-shaped boundaries that make a campaign family recognizable, governable, and reviewable across more than one asset.

That still keeps ingest concrete. The packet gives the seam chain declared boundaries rather than asking later stages to infer the campaign frame from aesthetic prose alone.

## Constraints Carried Across The Chain

The campaign packet acts as a normative constraint layer across transformation and output behavior. Campaign-frame continuity, bounded expressive range, required campaign identifiers, message-bearing signals, and source-bound limits should shape what later stages are allowed to produce, but they do not become runtime orchestration or generalized campaign schema by themselves.

This is the campaign-side counterpart to the furniture bridge, but the governing pressure is different. In the furniture case, product truth and fidelity carried much of the load. Here, continuity of the campaign family, bounded interpretive range, and the persistence of required signals do more of that structural work.

The important point is that the looser creative stream still remains bounded. The bridge should make clear that campaign variation is allowed, but only within declared continuity and governance limits.

## Output Expectations

At the output stage, the campaign packet informs whether governed outputs read as campaign-coherent, directionally bounded, and usable across the intended small set of surfaces. The relevant question is not whether every output looks the same, but whether the set remains recognizably part of the same campaign family while preserving the required campaign identifiers, message-bearing elements, and other review-relevant signals.

This also sharpens what sufficiency means in the campaign-side case. Sufficient governed outputs are not just aesthetically plausible images. They are outputs that hold together as one governed campaign family and remain usable enough to proceed toward review and release.

## Release-Readiness Expectations

At release-readiness, the campaign packet matters insofar as it helps determine whether the candidate set is ready to be presented for approval. The relevant question is whether the asset family remains within the intended campaign frame, keeps required signals legible enough, and presents its expressive variation in a way that is still coherent and reviewable.

Review artifacts are more explicit here than in the SKU-side packet because the campaign case carries more judgment-bearing variation. That does not automate approval. It simply makes campaign-side readiness more legible before the approval boundary is crossed.

## What This Does Not Do

- define a campaign brief system
- create a campaign style-spec
- formalize schema or field catalogs
- introduce runtime orchestration behavior
- define new seam logic or a new seam
- settle broader campaign taxonomy
